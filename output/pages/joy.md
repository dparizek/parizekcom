# Joy

This was never sent, was written to a hypothetical woman with an online dating profile... "So what's your story? What brings you joy?" ends her profile. If I wrote her, pitched to meet her, then ideally I'd want to address that, I think to myself. That would take a whole lot of words... I'd have to write her a book. That she'd likely not have time to read. Can I distill it? How? A poem would rock. I should learn how to write a poem. Bullet points? 50 of them? Ok. I could alternate between my story and my joys. I can’t sleep, rare insomnia, and writing helps there… hmm.. but if I actually send it to her she’ll think I’m nuts… 

  1. My Facebook intro says I live to witness the joie de vivre. I gain much joy simply in seeing the joy of others. Facebook dot com slash daveparizek. Not expecting reciprocation, sharing so that you can hopefully see I am a decent guy, safe to meet, and not crazy even though I am writing this.
  2. I was born in Tucson, but lived all over the US as a kid, and overseas in Singapore and Indonesia during high school.
  3. Being an honorable man brings me joy.
  4. I became a straight A student in like second grade, and thereafter, when my father offered to pay me for A’s.
  5. Nature brings me joy.
  6. I was a very entrepreneurial kid. I worked hard. My grades motivation aside, I am not overly driven by the desire for material wealth – more I enjoy that process.
  7. Rainy Saturday mornings bring me joy.
  8. I moved back to Tucson to attend the UA, planning to be pre-vet.
  9. When someone smiles - that brings me joy.
  10. I fell in love with conservation biology, and herpetology of all things, and got a BS in applied ecology, and an almost MS in the same.
  11. That triangle of her clavicle? – that brings me joy.
  12. Introverted and experiencing some culture shock being back in the US, with bad acne for awhile, and body image issues over being rather skinny, I did not date much in undergrad.
  13. A pug on my lap brings me joy.
  14. I grew out of that introversion in grad school, and then met the woman I was married for 18 years to.
  15. Ben and Jerry’s Chocolate Fudge ice cream… exquisite frozen joy.
  16. I finished all the deliverables for my MS, but never got it.
  17. Dresses bring me joy. When worn by women, not me, just to clarify. Although kilts look awfully damn comfortable.
  18. I did not go into ecology, but instead pursued various small businesses for most of my career to date. Day trader, bookseller, more.
  19. Giving back rubs brings me great joy. And receiving them too, of course.
  20. I was a stay home dad for eight years.
  21. Accomplishing something that I am not necessarily good at brings me joy. Like fixing something on my truck even though I am not really mechanically inclined.
  22. My marriage was dead for many years – we became roommates raising our kids together.
  23. Loving my sons, and trying my best to parent them well, brings me great joy.
  24. As my youngest approached kindergarten age, I knew my marriage would end, and I knew small business was not going to cut it now that I had kids. I needed something more on the secure and steady… I looked at teaching… but teachers have it so rough, especially in this state.
  25. Writing brings me joy. Even though much of my writing is “sappy?” like this, and usually not shared.
  26. I had always been good with computers, self-taught, and met a mentor who had more freelance work than she could handle. Having no resume given I was a stay home dad and had always worked for myself, I did a job for her for resume fodder. Made maybe $3 an hour fighting the learning curve, but got substance to build a resume.
  27. Watching an exceptional movie brings me joy, as does reading a captivating book.
  28. I went to volunteer at the UA to build that resume further but got hired into a paid position on the spot.
  29. Music brings me joy.
  30. I flourished and got promoted to a senior position 5 months later. Six years later I make twice what I did when I started. I worried, slightly, while stay home dad, that any career might suffer, but I got to have my cake and eat it too.
  31. All art brings me joy really.
  32. Divorced at 46 and never having really dated – I had to jump in. I wanted connection.
  33. Solving some tricky software programming issue brings me joy.
  34. I’ve dated a ton… and still have not found a life partner. Close, but timing, or life. So much growth from it though.
  35. When she looks at you a certain way, and it makes you melt, that brings me joy.
  36. I am an optimist, and will remain that. I have come to terms though that life will bring whatever it will bring. I am content – my life is full… my sons, my friends, my career… I am deeply blessed. Still I will keep looking for her even if I have to meet 500 women.
  37. Sometimes naps bring me joy.
  38. Love can be defined sooo many ways. Safe secure connection is how a scientist might. I believe there are two aspects that matter… the pitter patter, and the substance. The substance is shared values, looking outwards together, the critical trust, the ability to be emotionally available, and the desire/commitment over time to make it work. Some would say the pitter patter doesn’t matter… it fades… it is Hollywood rom coms they might say. I think the pitter patter still matters… yes it can fade, some, especially if not revered, but when it gets hard, and you can remember back that she was the one who took your breath away once upon a time, then that can be the thread that can get you to work all the harder when times get rough. In my dating, my story, I have been blessed to often find one or the other, but unfortunately not both in the same person. So I continue the quest.
  39. Exploring brings me joy. Exploring back roads with my sons four-wheeling. Exploring new concepts. Discovering the universe.
  40. Looking forward, my career is good. I will do meaningful side projects and maybe one will become a side business to be nurtured from the safely of my UA position. Regardless, it will be something that improves the world, so if it does not make money, no worries, still worthwhile.
  41. International exploring can be even sweeter joy.
  42. I will raise and cherish my sons.
  43. I’ve said it already, but using two to emphasize… my sons bring me joy.
  44. I want to travel more, and I want my sons to do so as well. I want them to be exposed to other cultures before they are out the door, as I was in high school. And hopefully there will be a she who was meant to travel with me.
  45. Water brings me joy.
  46. Life is short. I don’t want to waste any of my days, but neither do I want to be so caught up in doing that I don’t spend time simply being.
  47. Learning brings me joy.
  48. Onwards, opportunity will knock, and the best laid plans of mice and men… my future story I can really only guess at, so why am I trying to guess it?
  49. Life brings me joy.
  50. I think you should meet me even if my having written this is kind of out there, and for sure, over the top. I’m 100% normal in public (-: We could meet for coffee (a new found joy for me) and if nothing else, you’d have a new story.
