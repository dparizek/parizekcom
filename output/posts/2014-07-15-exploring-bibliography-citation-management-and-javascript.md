Where did FreeCite come from?

FreeCite was developed through a partnership between the [Brown University Library](<http://dl.lib.brown.edu/libweb/index.php>) and Providence-based start-up [Public Display](<http://www.pubdisplay.com/>), with funding from the [Mellon Foundation](<http://www.mellon.org/>). FreeCite was inspired by [ParsCit](<http://aye.comp.nus.edu.sg/parsCit/>). Much thanks to ParsCit's maintainer, Isaac Councill.

FreeCite is implemented in [Ruby on Rails](<http://www.rubyonrails.org/>) and uses the [CRF++ library](<http://crfpp.sourceforge.net/>) implementation of conditional random fields. Our model is trained on the [CORA dataset](<http://www.cs.umass.edu/~mccallum/data/cora-ie.tar.gz>) with lexical augmentation from the [Directory of Research and Researchers at Brown (DRR-B)](<http://research.brown.edu/research/search.php>).
